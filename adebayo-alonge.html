From Near Death to Saving Lives: The Story of Adebayo Alonge

From Near Death to Saving Lives: The Story of Adebayo Alonge

When Adebayo Alonge was just a teenager, he faced something most of us can’t imagine. He took medicine for his asthma—medicine his family believed would help—but instead, it almost killed him. The drugs were fake. His body shut down, and he was rushed to the hospital. For days, his family feared the worst.

That experience changed him forever. He survived, but he carried a painful truth with him: counterfeit drugs are a silent killer. Every year, hundreds of thousands of people in Africa lose their lives to fake medicines. They go to pharmacies with hope, but what they get is poison.

Adebayo could have moved on, buried the memory, and tried to forget. But he didn’t. That near-death experience became the fuel for his life’s mission. He wanted to make sure no one else had to suffer what he went through.

The Problem He Chose to Solve

Fake drugs are not just a Nigerian problem—they are a global one. In fact, the World Health Organization estimates that 1 in 10 medicines in low- and middle-income countries are fake or substandard. That’s millions of lives at risk.

Adebayo saw this problem up close. Families losing loved ones. Patients not recovering because the medicine they trusted was a lie. And he asked himself a simple but powerful question: “What if we could stop this?”

The Breakthrough

Fast forward years later, after studying pharmacy in Nigeria and pursuing further education at Yale and Stanford, Adebayo co-founded RxAll Inc. There, he and his team built something revolutionary: the RxScanner.

Think of it as a truth machine. A small handheld device that can instantly tell you if a pill is real or fake. No lab, no waiting, no guessing. Just a scan, and the truth is revealed.

This device means pharmacies, hospitals, and even governments can fight back against counterfeit drugs in real time. It means lives saved, families protected, and trust restored in medicine.

The Recognition

In 2019, Adebayo stood on a global stage in Paris at the Hello Tomorrow Global Challenge, competing against more than 4,500 startups from 119 countries. His mission was simple: show the world how RxScanner could end the plague of fake drugs.

And he won. He became the first African to ever win the grand prize at the competition. But beyond the trophy and headlines, what mattered most was this: the world was now listening.

The Impact

Today, RxScanner is being used across Africa and beyond. Every time it detects a fake drug, it potentially saves a life. Every hospital that adopts it reduces the risk of tragedy. The impact may not always make the news, but it is real, powerful, and ongoing.

For Adebayo, this is not just about technology. It’s about dignity. About making sure that when a mother buys medicine for her child, she gets healing, not heartbreak.

Why His Story Matters

Adebayo’s story is proof that pain can become purpose. He turned a personal tragedy into a solution for millions. He teaches us that the problems we survive can sometimes point us to the missions we were born to solve.

And here’s the lesson for all of us: you don’t have to invent a scanner or win a global prize to make a difference. You just have to care enough about a problem to start. That’s how changemakers are born.

Giving Him His Flowers

Spotlite Africa celebrates Adebayo Alonge not just for his brilliance, but for his courage. He took his scar and turned it into a shield for others. He is saving lives, restoring trust, and showing the world that Africa is not only a place of challenges, but a birthplace of solutions.

So the next time you think one person cannot change the world, remember this: a teenager who almost died from fake medicine grew up to build the tool that could end counterfeit drugs forever.

That’s not just impact—that’s legacy.
